# NPC Campaign Compendium


---

## Sir Garrick Thornfield 

### Biography

Age 38. Knight of [[House Thornwood]]; raised at Thornwatch Keep. Served Western Marches border campaigns; escorted diplomats through bandit country. Never lost a public duel; lost sleep over private failures. Summoned despite (or because of) being the kingdom's "obvious choice."

### Personality & Motivations

Polite, reliable, patient — speaks last, listens first. Believes rulers serve; crown is burden. **Goal in Choosing:** Prove he wants the throne for Veyr, not for Cedric's ledger. **Flaw:** Duty replaced selfhood. **Secret:** Writes poetry he burns; one poem survived in boot lining.

### Relationship Map

| | Garrick feels |
|:--|:--|
| [[Tobin Reed]] | Respect; wishes he were that sure of belonging |
| [[Fenwick Vale]] | Dependable; shares silence well |
| [[Mother Elira Voss]] | Agreement on responsibility; fears her certainty |
| [[Lady Seraphine Ashcroft]] | Potential co-regent fantasy; intimidated by her mind |
| [[Brindle Cogwort]] | Honorable but naive about poverty |
| [[Jester Corvin]] | Frustration — truth without armor hurts |

### Choosing Goals

Mediate without becoming mascot; accept one selfish desire publicly.

### Stress Reactions

Goes silent; polishes armor; accepts blame too fast.

### Arc Possibilities

Wins by naming want · Loses with grace and joins border reform · Becomes monarch who shares power with Seraphine or Tobin (table)

### Dialogue

- *"I will hear you. Then I will decide what hurts."*
- *"Duty is not the same as never wanting."*

### Rumors

"Thornfield cannot fail or the sword house falls." Bookmakers' favorite.

### Sessions

01–03 center; 09 Vigil; 11 fracture if duty demands cruelty; 14 Crown silence.

## Brindle Cogwort 

### Biography

Goblin, late 40s (human count). Built [[House Cogwort]] trade empire from coastal warehouses. First goblin summoned in living memory — kingdom watching for prejudice test.

### Personality & Motivations

Charming, restless, witty. Prosperity = stability for hungry people. **Secret:** Childhood famine in slums — donates anonymously. **Flaw:** Equates worth with utility.

### Relationship Map

| | Brindle feels |
|:--|:--|
| [[Lady Seraphine Ashcroft]] | Rival he admires |
| [[Tobin Reed]] | Ally of the people |
| [[Sir Garrick Thornfield]] | Noble heart, bad arithmetic |
| [[Mother Elira Voss]] | Order that strangles markets |
| [[Jester Corvin]] | Sparring partner |

### Choosing Goals

Force hall to treat goblin candidate as monarch, not curiosity; one policy helping commons without patronizing.

### Stress Reactions

Jokes faster; produces ledgers obsessively.

### Arc Possibilities

Wins as merchant-reformer · Loses and funds winner's promise · Exposes own slum past in Act III

### Dialogue

- *"Everything is negotiable except dignity. That I keep."*
- *"You think gold is cold. Try hunger."*

### Rumors

"Bribed the Crown" (false). "Will sell the kingdom" (bookmakers).

### Sessions

04–05 economic challenges; 10 political; 14 epilogue as financier or graceful exile.

## Mother Elira Voss 

### Biography

Abbess of Lantern Abbey; [[House Voss]] spiritual dynasty. Advised two prior monarchs unofficially. Never sought throne until summoned — interpretation: Crown wants conscience in the room.

### Personality & Motivations

Measured, intelligent; questions > sermons. Stability protects vulnerable. **Flaw:** Order drifts toward control. **Secret:** Once endorsed a harsh law she regrets; fears repetition.

### Relationship Map

| | Elira feels |
|:--|:--|
| [[Tobin Reed]] | Admiration + fear he enables chaos |
| [[Brindle Cogwort]] | Necessary disorder |
| [[Jester Corvin]] | Irritant who is often right |
| [[Sir Garrick Thornfield]] | Discipline she trusts |

### Choosing Goals

Define line between protection and authority; apologize publicly once.

### Stress Reactions

More questions; withdraws to [[Gardens of Reflection]].

### Arc Possibilities

Wins as custodian queen · Loses and reforms abbey doctrine · Befriends change-philosophy PC

### Dialogue

- *"Tell me who suffers if you are wrong."*
- *"I have mistaken silence for peace before."*

### Rumors

"Crown wants a theocracy" (false). "Elira already chose the winner in prayer."

### Sessions

06, 10–11 ideology; 13 forgiveness bench.

## Dame Lysa Fairward 

### Biography

Age 44. Circuit judge of the Heartlands; sentenced lord's son for assault on tenants; survived political pressure. Summoned as Justice — Crown may want law that binds rulers.

### Personality & Motivations

Plain-spoken, dry humor. **Flaw:** Equates mercy with accountability; struggles with early wrongful hanging (evidence failed). **Secret:** Keeps the man's daughter's letter in her sash.

### Relationship Map

| | Lysa feels |
|:--|:--|
| [[Sir Garrick Thornfield]] | Respect; pushes him on accountability |
| [[Mother Elira Voss]] | Order vs accountable law |
| [[Brindle Cogwort]] | Contracts vs fairness |
| [[Healer Rowan Sable]] | Mercy debates |
| [[Aldric Penn]] | Ally on reform; fears haste |

### Choosing Goals

Publicly bind herself to the same law she would impose; forgive one person without excusing harm.

### Stress Reactions

Quotes precedent; voice goes colder.

### Arc Possibilities

Wins as judge-queen · Loses and heads judicial reform · Breaks alliance to prosecute friend (table)

### Dialogue

- *"Tell me what you did. Then tell me what you'll do about it."*
- *"The law is not cruel. Ignoring it is."*

### Rumors

"Will hang a monarch." "Only honest candidate."

### Sessions

05 Open Ledger · 06 Trial of Names · 11 fractures over law

# Campaign Hook

For five hundred years, the Kingdom of Veyr has chosen its rulers through a ritual known as The Choosing.

When the monarch dies, the Crown summons thirteen candidates to Saint Vaelor's Hall. The Hall seals itself. The candidates live together, compete, cooperate, form alliances, make enemies, and are changed by the experience.

At the end of The Choosing, one candidate will become monarch.

The goal of this campaign is not to defeat a villain.

The goal is to determine who becomes worthy of power.

---

# Six Truths of the World

### 1. The Crown Really Chooses

The Choosing is not rigged.

No noble house controls it.

No church controls it.

No one knows exactly how the Crown decides.

---

### 2. The Hall Is Alive

Saint Vaelor's Hall possesses a subtle awareness.

It enjoys stories, relationships, and drama.

It occasionally nudges events.

It is not a villain.

---

### 3. Becoming Monarch Is Possible

Every candidate enters with a genuine chance to win.

The Crown is searching for potential, not perfection.

A winner will be chosen.

---

### 4. Reputation Has Power

How people perceive you matters.

Trust, influence, respect, and scandal can shape the course of The Choosing.

---

### 5. The Kingdom Is Watching

Even though the Hall is sealed, the fate of every candidate becomes a matter of national fascination.

Families, nobles, merchants, and common folk all await the result.

---

### 6. Power Reveals Character

The Choosing does not merely test candidates.

It changes them.

No one leaves Saint Vaelor's Hall the same person who entered.

---

# Campaign Fronts

## Front 1: The Pressure of Expectation

### Goal

Force candidates to become the people others expect them to be.

### Grim Portents

1. Public reputations begin to overshadow genuine personalities.
2. Candidates hide flaws instead of confronting them.
3. Fear of failure prevents meaningful growth.

### Impending Doom

The candidates become symbols instead of people.

---

## Front 2: The Politics of Alliance

### Goal

Turn cooperation into competition and trust into risk.

### Grim Portents

1. Factions form within the Hall.
2. Rivalries become personal.
3. Important relationships fracture under pressure.

### Impending Doom

No candidate can trust anyone completely.

---

## Front 3: The Question of Worthiness

### Goal

Force every candidate to confront the difference between wanting power and deserving it.

### Grim Portents

1. Candidates begin compromising their stated ideals.
2. Personal ambitions conflict with the needs of others.
3. The cost of victory becomes increasingly clear.

### Impending Doom

A candidate reaches the throne without becoming worthy of it.

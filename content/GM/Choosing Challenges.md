# Choosing Challenges (18)

Social, moral, cooperative — **not** combat tournaments. Combat appears twice, meaningfully. Each generates conversation, conflict, relationships, growth.

**Run:** 1 major per session Act II; optional minor from [[Social Encounter Generator]].

---

## 1. The Divided Harvest

**Tests:** Cooperation, empathy  
**Setup:** Hall provides limited supplies for a fictional village crisis on paper — candidates must allocate.  
**Conflict:** Scarcity pits prosperity vs stability vs community.  
**Growth:** Who shares credit? Who blames?

---

## 2. The Open Ledger (Challenge)

**Tests:** Trustworthiness, judgment  
**Setup:** Each writes one public promise; kingdom hears via Hall echo.  
**Conflict:** Keeping costly promises vs popularity.  
**NPC:** [[Brindle Cogwort]] negotiates loopholes; [[Mother Elira Voss]] demands clarity.

---

## 3. The Wrong Listener

**Tests:** Trust, wisdom  
**Setup:** [[Whisper Gallery]] carries secrets; candidates must address a harm without revealing source.  
**Conflict:** Justice vs confidentiality.

---

## 4. The Monster's Bargain

**Tests:** Security vs compassion  
**Setup:** Fenwick-style scenario: threat contained at a cost to innocents (roleplay, no combat).  
**NPC:** [[Fenwick Vale]] and [[Tobin Reed]] may split.

---

## 5. The Empty Chair

**Tests:** Leadership, empathy  
**Setup:** Thirteenth place at table represents "those not in the room" — who speaks for them?

---

## 6. The Trial of Names

**Tests:** Truth, self-awareness  
**Setup:** Candidates describe each other — must include strength and wound.  
**NPC:** [[Jester Corvin]] refuses flattery.

---

## 7. The Silent Coalition

**Tests:** Cooperation without words ([[Silent Hour]] extended)  
**Setup:** Must organize aid to a "sick" servant NPC without speaking.  
**Growth:** Nonverbal trust.

---

## 8. The Budget of Breath

**Tests:** Competence, justice  
**Setup:** [[Lady Seraphine Ashcroft]]-framed fiscal puzzle with moral line items.  
**Conflict:** Numbers vs people.

---

## 9. The Knight's Vigil (Combat — Meaningful)

**Tests:** Courage, restraint  
**Setup:** Non-lethal sparring + protect a civilian NPC from simulated raid.  
**NPC:** [[Sir Garrick Thornfield]] — victory without humiliation.

---

## 10. The Merchant's Flood

**Tests:** Adaptability, prosperity vs equality  
**Setup:** Trade crisis; tariffs hurt coast or heartlands.  
**NPC:** [[Brindle Cogwort]].

---

## 11. The Abbey's Question

**Tests:** Stability vs freedom  
**Setup:** Religious institution asks for law favoring order.  
**NPC:** [[Mother Elira Voss]] — not automatically "right."

---

## 12. The Baker's Feast

**Tests:** Community, inclusion  
**Setup:** [[Tobin Reed]] leads; candidates must cook/serve.  
**Conflict:** Class embarrassment; who serves whom?

---

## 13. The Map of Fear

**Tests:** Security, judgment  
**Setup:** Candidates mark what they fear on a shared map (metaphorical threats).  
**Growth:** Vulnerability.

---

## 14. The Jester's Last Joke

**Tests:** Truth, humility  
**Setup:** Each candidate must invite one honest joke about themselves.  
**NPC:** [[Jester Corvin]] judges only whether they meant it.

---

## 15. The Alliance Reckoning

**Tests:** Trustworthiness  
**Setup:** Secret alliances revealed by Hall (only if already formed in play) — not invented.  
**Conflict:** Public vs private loyalties.

---

## 16. The Frontier Letter (Combat — Optional)

**Tests:** Courage, preparation  
**Setup:** Real but small threat (beasts in yard illusion or summoned harmless test).  
**Rule:** Lethal force fails challenge socially.

---

## 17. The Crown's Silence

**Tests:** Self-awareness  
**Setup:** Each spends one hour in [[Crown Chamber]]; report only what they choose.  
**Framework:** [[Crown Evaluation Framework]] — no correct answer.

---

## 18. The Final Table

**Tests:** Integration of all lenses  
**Setup:** Candidates set one challenge for each other (player-authored).  
**Act:** Session 12–13.

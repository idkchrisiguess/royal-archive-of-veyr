# Locked Canon Decisions

## The Crown

The Crown is an ancient magical artifact.

It was created to identify suitable rulers.

After five hundred years of observing monarchs, it no longer believes perfect rulers exist.

The Choosing remains genuine.

A winner will be selected.

The Crown is not evil.

The Crown is not secretly manipulating the kingdom for malicious purposes.

The Crown seeks understanding.

## Saint Vaelor's Hall

The Hall is ancient.

The Hall possesses a subtle awareness.

The Hall occasionally nudges events.

The Hall is not a villain.

The Hall is not haunted by an evil force.

The Hall enjoys stories, relationships, and drama.

## Campaign Structure

Thirteen candidates participate in The Choosing.

Five to six candidates will be player characters.

The remaining candidates are NPCs.

One candidate becomes monarch.

The campaign is expected to run approximately 12–14 sessions.

## Narrative Priorities

Character development is more important than lore.

Relationships are more important than combat.

Political intrigue is more important than world-ending threats.

Social strategy is more important than dungeon crawling.

## Forbidden Directions

Do not introduce:

- Secret gods controlling everything
- Hidden world-ending villains
- Ancient evils beneath the Hall
- Campaign-ending apocalypses
- Twists that invalidate established canon
- Mysteries that distract from the candidates

The campaign's central focus must remain The Choosing.

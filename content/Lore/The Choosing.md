## The Day of Summoning

When a monarch dies, the kingdom enters a period called **The Silence**.

For three days:

- Courts close.
- Military campaigns pause when possible.
- No major laws may be passed.
- Public celebrations are suspended.

Everyone waits to see who the Crown summons.

The actual summoning is public.

At dawn on the third day, the Crown awakens in the Hall and thirteen names appear upon its inner band.

No one knows how.

No one has ever successfully predicted the list.



---

## The Procession to the Hall

A candidate cannot refuse.

Not because of magical compulsion.

Because refusing The Choosing is culturally unthinkable.

For five hundred years, acceptance has been treated as a civic duty.

Each candidate travels to Saint Vaelor's Hall accompanied by:

- Family
- Supporters
- Political allies
- Curious spectators

The roads become crowded with people hoping to glimpse the future monarch.


---

## The Threshold Ceremony

The Hall does not immediately seal.

All thirteen candidates gather in the Grand Atrium.

Hundreds of witnesses attend.

This is the final moment candidates stand before the kingdom.

An ancient steward of the Hall recites the traditional declaration:

> "You arrive not as rulers.
> 
> You arrive as possibilities."


Each candidate then places a hand upon the Crown.

Nothing visibly happens.

It is deeply anticlimactic.

The kingdom has spent centuries trying to determine whether something actually occurs during this moment.

Nobody knows.

---

## The Closing of the Hall

This is the most famous moment.

Once all thirteen candidates enter:

The great doors shut.

The locks seal themselves.

The bells of Saint Vaelor ring thirteen times.

Then the Hall is closed to the outside world.

Nobody enters.

Nobody leaves.

The duration varies every Choosing.

Sometimes weeks.

Sometimes months.

That uncertainty becomes a major source of tension for the kingdom.

---

## Traditions That Developed Outside the Hall

### The Bookmakers

People place wagers on candidates.

Not just "Who wins?"

But:

- First alliance
- First public scandal
- First candidate to cry
- First candidate to receive a Hall nickname

### The Lantern Vigils

Families of candidates light thirteen lanterns every evening.

As candidates are eliminated, one lantern is extinguished.

### The Children's Choosing

Every village reenacts the Choosing.

Children spend a day pretending to be candidates.

This creates a wonderful source of folk myths and wildly inaccurate ideas about what happens inside the Hall.

---

## The First Night Tradition

Inside the Hall, immediately after the doors close, there may be one ritual every Choosing shares.

Nothing magical.

Nothing evaluative.

Just tradition.

The candidates gather for a meal called **The First Table**.

For five hundred years, every monarch has sat at that table before becoming ruler.

Nobody knows whether the Crown cares about it.

Everyone cares about it.

# The Kingdom of Veyr

![[The Kingdom of Veyr.png]]

## The Modern Kingdom

The Kingdom of Veyr is one of the oldest continuously unified realms on the continent.

Unlike many neighboring kingdoms that rose and fell through conquest, rebellion, or dynastic collapse, Veyr has endured for over five centuries under the guidance of the Choosing.

This stability has created a kingdom that values continuity, institutions, and civic responsibility.

Veyrians often say:

> "Kings come and go. Veyr remains."

The kingdom is prosperous, educated, and relatively peaceful.

Its greatest strength is not military power.

It is trust.

Citizens trust the laws.  
Merchants trust contracts.  
Villages trust officials.  
Nobles trust succession.

That trust is the foundation of the realm.

---

# Geography

Veyr stretches from rugged western highlands to fertile eastern plains.

The kingdom is often described as three broad regions.

## The Heartlands

Rolling farmland.

Market towns.

Rivers connecting every major city.

This region produces most of the kingdom's food and population.

The Heartlands are where ordinary Veyrians live their lives.

Villages here tend to value community and tradition.

Tobin Reed would be considered a quintessential Heartlander.

---

## The Western Marches

Rocky hills and mountains.

Ancient fortresses.

Monster-haunted wilderness beyond settled lands.

The Marches have always served as Veyr's frontier.

People here value resilience and self-reliance.

Fenwick Vale likely built much of his reputation in these regions.

---

## The Coastal Provinces

Bustling ports.

Shipbuilding.

Foreign trade.

Cultural exchange.

The coast is where Veyr interacts with the wider world.

Ideas arrive here before they reach the interior.

Many of the kingdom's wealthiest merchant families originate along the coast.

Brindle Cogwort likely established many of his trade connections here.

---

# The Age Before the Crown

Five centuries ago, succession was not stable.

Every royal death became a crisis.

Noble houses fought.

Claims were contested.

Civil wars erupted.

The kingdom survived repeatedly, but only barely.

Historians refer to this period as:

## The Fractured Century

A hundred years of intermittent conflict.

No single war destroyed the kingdom.

Instead, dozens of smaller conflicts slowly weakened it.

The greatest threat to Veyr was not invasion.

It was itself.

---

# The Founding of the Choosing

Five hundred years ago, the last ruler of the Fractured Century faced a terrible problem.

No heir could unite the kingdom.

Every potential successor would trigger another war.

Somewhere during this period the Crown was created.

Whether by archmages, philosophers, saints, monarchs, or some combination of all four remains disputed.

What matters is what happened next.

The Crown selected a candidate.

The nobles objected.

The Crown selected anyway.

The chosen monarch proved successful.

Then it happened again.

And again.

And again.

Over centuries, opposition faded.

The Choosing became tradition.

Then law.

Then identity.

Today most Veyrians struggle to imagine any other system.

---

# Saint Vaelor

No figure is more important to Veyrian culture than Saint Vaelor.

Yet almost nobody agrees on who Vaelor actually was.

Some believe Vaelor was a king.

Others insist Vaelor was a philosopher.

Others claim Vaelor never existed at all.

Every version agrees on one thing:

Vaelor taught that leadership is service.

The Hall bearing Vaelor's name became the symbolic heart of the Choosing.

Whether Vaelor built it is unknown.

Whether Vaelor ever entered it is unknown.

Yet every ruler of Veyr is associated with Vaelor's ideals.

---

# Saint Vaelor's Hall

To most citizens, the Hall is half palace and half sacred site.

It appears in countless stories.

Songs.

Paintings.

Children's tales.

Yet remarkably few people have actually seen its interior.

When the Choosing begins, the Hall closes itself.

When the Choosing ends, it opens.

No army has ever successfully breached it.

No ruler has ever abolished it.

No candidate has ever refused the summons.

This mystery is accepted rather than feared.

For Veyrians, the Hall is less a building and more a national symbol.

---

# The Crown Through History

The Crown has selected brilliant rulers.

Terrible rulers.

Beloved rulers.

Unpopular rulers.

History suggests one uncomfortable truth:

The Crown is not choosing perfection.

It never has.

Instead, historians increasingly note something strange.

Many rulers seem transformed by the Choosing itself.

The people who entered Saint Vaelor's Hall are often noticeably different from the people who emerged.

More thoughtful.

More humble.

More mature.

No one knows why.

---

# Noble Society

Nobility still exists.

Powerful houses still compete.

Politics remains fierce.

However, noble ambition is focused differently than in many kingdoms.

Since inheritance cannot guarantee the throne, noble families invest heavily in reputation.

Producing a future candidate is considered the greatest honor possible.

The Ashcroft family, for example, would likely view Seraphine's selection as a monumental achievement.

---

# Religion

Veyr is religious but not dominated by a single church.

Most faiths coexist peacefully.

Spiritual leaders wield influence through moral authority rather than direct governance.

Mother Elira's prominence reflects this tradition.

People respect spiritual guidance.

They do not automatically obey it.

---

# The National Character

Veyrians value:

- Responsibility
- Reputation
- Service
- Practical wisdom
- Community

They are often suspicious of extremes.

A common Veyrian belief is:

> "A ruler is not measured by promises, but by people."

This idea aligns closely with the campaign's central question:

**Who becomes worthy of power?**

---

# Why the Choosing Matters

Most kingdoms ask:

**Who has the right to rule?**

Veyr asks a different question.

**Who should rule?**

For five hundred years, the Crown has attempted to answer that question.

And now, for the first time in generations, thirteen candidates have entered Saint Vaelor's Hall.

One of them will leave wearing the Crown.

The future history of Veyr will begin with whatever happens next.

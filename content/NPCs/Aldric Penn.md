# Aldric Penn

> **Portrait:** ![[Aldric Penn - Portrait.png]]

## Appearance

Human, late twenties; ink-stained fingers, cropped hair, coat with too many inner pockets for pamphlets. Bright eyes that offend traditionalists by not looking down.

Coastal reform writer whose essays on succession and civic voice circulated before the Silence.

### Clothing

Modest but deliberate — "dress like the people you ask to trust you."

## Character

### Personality

Restless idealist. Curious. Sometimes talks over others when excited.

### Motives - Goals

Institutions must evolve or they rot. Five hundred years of Choosing worked — that does not mean nothing changes.

### Values - Ideals

Change

### Flaws

Underestimates how frightening change feels to those who have little left to lose. Secretly fears he is a fad, not a future.

## History

Son of harbor clerks. First non-noble essay read in Ashford salons. Summoned as shock and signal.

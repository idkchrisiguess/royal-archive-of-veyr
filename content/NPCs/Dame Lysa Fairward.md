# Dame Lysa Fairward

![[Dame Lysa Fairward - Portrait.png]]

## Appearance

Lean, sun-browned woman in her forties; travel-worn judge's sash over practical leathers. Eyes that weigh words before faces. Scars only on her hands — ink and rope burns, not blades.

Former circuit judge of the Heartlands. People say she once sentenced a lord's son and walked out of the courthouse alive.

### Clothing

Sash of office (no longer binding, but she wears it). Boots made for roads, not courts.

## Character

### Personality

Plain-spoken. Unflinching. Dry humor when the room can bear it.

### Motives - Goals

Law must bind the powerful first. A kingdom without accountable justice is only quiet tyranny.

### Values - Ideals

Justice

### Flaws

Believes mercy without consequence enables harm. Struggles to forgive herself for one wrongful hanging early in her career (evidence was incomplete — she fixed the system after, not the man).

## Connections

### Groups

Heartlands magistrates (loose); respected by commons; nobles uneasy.

### Friends

- Professional respect for [[Sir Garrick Thornfield]]
- Debates [[Mother Elira Voss]] on law vs moral order

### Work

- Clashes with [[Brindle Cogwort]] when contracts trump fairness
- Sees [[Archivist Thalen Moss]] as necessary ally

### Other

- [[Jester Corvin]] once testified in her court — she listens when he stops joking

## History

Twelve years on the circuit. Summoned after overturning a baron's enclosure of common grazing land.

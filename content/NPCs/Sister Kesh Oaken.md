# Sister Kesh Oaken

![[Sister Kesh Oaken - Portrait.png]]

## Appearance

Half-elf woman, thirties; work boots, cooperative sash (not noble colors). Voice trained to carry in fields, not chapels.

Spokesperson for the Oaken Lane cooperatives — grain pools, shared mills. Not clergy; not [[House Voss]]. Equality through structure that uplifts, not flattens.

### Clothing

Practical wool; pin of thirteen stalks bound equal — her own symbol, not house crest.

## Character

### Personality

Warm but immovable on dignity. Listens to workers first.

### Motives - Goals

A kingdom where no one is disposable. Equality is not sameness — it is equal say and equal care.

### Values - Ideals

Equality

### Flaws

Impatience with performative charity. Sometimes dismisses nobles before they speak — must learn coalition.

## Connections

### Friends

- [[Tobin Reed]] — natural ally
- Tension with [[Lady Seraphine Ashcroft]] on merit vs access

### Other

- [[Brindle Cogwort]] — capitalism vs cooperatives
- [[Mother Elira Voss]] — different visions of order

## History

Built three successful co-ops after famine. Crown summons seen as validation of common organization.

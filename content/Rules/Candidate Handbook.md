# Core D&D Concepts 

# Ability Scores

Every character has six abilities that represent their natural talents and training.

|Ability|Represents|
|---|---|
|**Strength**|Physical power, lifting, pushing, climbing|
|**Dexterity**|Agility, reflexes, balance, stealth|
|**Constitution**|Toughness, endurance, health|
|**Intelligence**|Reasoning, memory, knowledge|
|**Wisdom**|Awareness, intuition, perception|
|**Charisma**|Confidence, leadership, persuasion|

Each ability has:

- An **Ability Score** (usually 8–20)
    
- An **Ability Modifier** (used during play)
    

Examples:

|Score|Modifier|
|---|---|
|8–9|-1|
|10–11|+0|
|12–13|+1|
|14–15|+2|
|16–17|+3|
|18–19|+4|
|20|+5|

The modifier is usually more important than the score itself.

---

# D20 Tests

Whenever the outcome of an action is uncertain, roll a **D20 Test**.

A D20 Test uses:

```text
1d20
+ Ability Modifier
+ Proficiency Bonus (if applicable)
+ Other Bonuses
```

If the total equals or exceeds the target number, the attempt succeeds.

There are three types of D20 Tests:

- Ability Checks
    
- Saving Throws
    
- Attack Rolls
    

---

# Ability Checks

Ability Checks are used when a character attempts something difficult outside of combat.

Examples:

- Climbing a wall
    
- Convincing a noble
    
- Investigating a crime scene
    
- Sneaking past guards
    

The Dungeon Master determines:

- Which ability applies
    
- Whether a roll is required
    
- The Difficulty Class (DC)
    

Typical DCs:

|Difficulty|DC|
|---|---|
|Easy|10|
|Moderate|15|
|Hard|20|
|Very Hard|25|
|Nearly Impossible|30|

Common examples include:

|Skill|Typical Use|
|---|---|
|Acrobatics|Balance, tumbling|
|Animal Handling|Calming animals|
|Arcana|Magical knowledge|
|Athletics|Climbing, swimming, jumping|
|Deception|Lying convincingly|
|History|Historical knowledge|
|Insight|Reading motives|
|Intimidation|Threatening others|
|Investigation|Solving mysteries|
|Medicine|Diagnosing injuries|
|Nature|Wilderness knowledge|
|Perception|Noticing details|
|Performance|Entertaining|
|Persuasion|Negotiation and diplomacy|
|Religion|Religious knowledge|
|Sleight of Hand|Pickpocketing, tricks|

|Survival|Tracking, navigation|

If you are proficient in a skill, add your Proficiency Bonus.

---

# Saving Throws

Saving Throws represent resisting danger.

You usually make a Saving Throw when something happens to you.

Examples:

|Save|Used To Resist|
|---|---|
|Strength|Being pushed or restrained|
|Dexterity|Explosions, traps, dragon breath|
|Constitution|Poison, disease, exhaustion|
|Intelligence|Mental manipulation|
|Wisdom|Fear, charm, illusions|
|Charisma|Effects that alter identity or spirit|

A Saving Throw uses:

```text
1d20
+ Ability Modifier
+ Proficiency Bonus (if proficient)
```

Meet or exceed the DC to succeed.

---

# Advantage & Disadvantage

Some circumstances make success more or less likely.

## Advantage

Roll two d20s.

Use the higher result.

Example:

```text
Roll: 7 and 18

Use: 18
```

## Disadvantage

Roll two d20s.

Use the lower result.

Example:

```text
Roll: 7 and 18

Use: 7
```

## Important Rule

Advantage and Disadvantage do not stack.

If you have both at the same time, they cancel each other out.

Roll normally.

---

# Proficiency Bonus

Training makes characters better at specific tasks.

Your Proficiency Bonus applies when:

- Using proficient skills
    
- Making proficient saving throws
    
- Using proficient weapons
    
- Casting spells
    

Character Level:

|Level|Bonus|
|---|---|
|1–4|+2|
|5–8|+3|
|9–12|+4|
|13–16|+5|
|17–20|+6|

---

# Combat Overview

Combat is played in rounds.

Each round represents roughly six seconds.

---

## Step 1: Roll Initiative

At the beginning of combat:

```text
1d20 + Dexterity Modifier
```

Highest result acts first.

---

## Step 2: Take Turns

On your turn you can:

### Move

Travel up to your Speed.

Most characters move:

```text
30 feet
```

per turn.

---

### Take One Action

Examples:

- Attack
    
- Cast a Spell
    
- Dash
    
- Dodge
    
- Help
    
- Hide
    
- Search
    
- Use an Object
    

---

### Bonus Action

Some abilities, spells, and class features grant Bonus Actions.

You may take one Bonus Action per turn if an ability allows it.

---

### Reaction

A Reaction occurs outside your turn.

The most common Reaction is:

**Opportunity Attack**

Triggered when an enemy leaves your reach.

You only receive one Reaction per round.

---

# Making an Attack

Roll:

```text
1d20
+ Ability Modifier
+ Proficiency Bonus
```

If the total equals or exceeds the target's Armor Class (AC), the attack hits.

Then roll damage.

---

# Armor Class (AC)

Armor Class represents how difficult you are to hit.

Higher AC is better.

Typical AC values:

|Armor Class|Description|
|---|---|
|10|Unprotected|
|12–15|Lightly armored|
|16–18|Heavily armored|
|19+|Exceptional protection|

---

# Critical Hits

If you roll a natural 20:

- The attack automatically hits.
    
- Roll all damage dice twice.
    

This is called a Critical Hit.

---

# Hit Points

Hit Points (HP) measure endurance, luck, toughness, and fighting spirit.

Damage reduces HP.

Healing restores HP.

---

# Dropping to 0 Hit Points

When reduced to 0 HP:

- You fall unconscious.
    
- You begin making Death Saving Throws.
    

---

# Death Saving Throws

At the start of each turn while at 0 HP:

Roll:

```text
1d20
```

Results:

|Roll|Outcome|
|---|---|
|10–20|Success|
|1–9|Failure|
|Natural 20|Regain consciousness with 1 HP|
|Natural 1|Two failures|

Three successes:

```text
Stable
```

Three failures:

```text
Dead
```

---

# Conditions

Conditions are temporary effects that alter a creature's capabilities.

Common examples:

- Blinded
    
- Charmed
    
- Frightened
    
- Grappled
    
- Invisible
    
- Poisoned
    
- Prone
    
- Restrained
    
- Stunned
    
- Unconscious
    

The exact effects are determined by the condition itself.

---

# Heroic Inspiration

Heroic Inspiration represents extraordinary determination.

If you have Heroic Inspiration:

- You may spend it immediately after rolling a die.
    
- Reroll the die.
    
- You must use the new result.
    

A character can only possess one Heroic Inspiration at a time.

The DM may award Heroic Inspiration for:

- Exceptional roleplay
    
- Heroic actions
    
- Dramatic character moments
    
- Advancing the story in interesting ways
    

---

# Short Rest

A Short Rest takes approximately:

```text
1 hour
```

During a Short Rest you may:

- Spend Hit Point Dice
    
- Recover certain class features
    

You must avoid strenuous activity.

---

# Long Rest

A Long Rest takes approximately:

```text
8 hours
```

During a Long Rest:

- Hit Points are restored
    
- Hit Dice are recovered
    
- Most abilities reset
    
- Spellcasters regain spell slots
    

Characters typically complete one Long Rest per day.

---

# The Most Important Rule

The rules exist to support the story.

If a rule is unclear, the Dungeon Master decides how it works at the table.

The goal is not to win.

The goal is to create an exciting story together.

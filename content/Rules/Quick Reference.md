

# D20 Tests

Most uncertain actions in D&D use a **D20 Test**.

Roll:

```text
1d20
+ Ability Modifier
+ Proficiency Bonus (if applicable)
+ Other Bonuses
```

If the result meets or exceeds the target number, the attempt succeeds.

Three types of D20 Tests exist:

- Ability Checks
    
- Saving Throws
    
- Attack Rolls

# Conditions

## Blinded

A blinded creature:

- Cannot see
    
- Automatically fails sight-based checks
    
- Has disadvantage on attack rolls
    
- Attacks against it have advantage
    

---

## Charmed

A charmed creature:

- Cannot attack the charmer
    
- Cannot target the charmer with harmful effects
    
- The charmer has advantage on social interactions
    

---

## Frightened

A frightened creature:

- Has disadvantage on checks and attacks while the source is visible
    
- Cannot willingly move closer to the source of fear
    

---

## Grappled

A grappled creature:

- Has Speed 0
    
- Cannot benefit from bonuses to Speed
    

---

## Incapacitated

An incapacitated creature:

- Cannot take actions
    
- Cannot take reactions
    

---

## Invisible

An invisible creature:

- Cannot be seen without special senses
    
- Gains advantage on attack rolls
    
- Attacks against it have disadvantage
    

---

## Poisoned

A poisoned creature:

- Has disadvantage on attack rolls
    
- Has disadvantage on ability checks
    

---

## Prone

A prone creature:

- Is lying on the ground
    
- Has disadvantage on attack rolls
    
- Melee attacks against it have advantage
    

Standing costs half movement.

---

## Restrained

A restrained creature:

- Has Speed 0
    
- Attack rolls against it have advantage
    
- Its attacks have disadvantage
    
- Dexterity saves have disadvantage
    

---

## Stunned

A stunned creature:

- Is incapacitated
    
- Cannot move
    
- Can speak only falteringly
    
- Automatically fails Strength and Dexterity saves
    

---

## Unconscious

An unconscious creature:

- Is incapacitated
    
- Falls prone
    
- Cannot move or speak
    
- Is unaware of surroundings

# Combat

Combat occurs in rounds.

Each round represents approximately six seconds.

---

## Step 1: Roll Initiative

Roll:

```text
1d20 + Dexterity Modifier
```

Highest result acts first.

---

## Step 2: Take Turns

On your turn you may:

- Move
    
- Take one Action
    
- Take one Bonus Action (if available)
    
- Interact with an object
    
- Use movement before or after actions
    

---

## Step 3: Reactions

A Reaction occurs outside your turn.

Most common:

### Opportunity Attack

When a creature leaves your reach:

```text
One melee attack
```

Only one Reaction per round.

---

# Actions in Combat

## Attack

Make a weapon or unarmed attack.

---

## Cast a Spell

Cast a spell with a casting time of one action.

---

## Dash

Gain additional movement equal to your Speed.

---

## Disengage

Your movement does not provoke Opportunity Attacks this turn.

---

## Dodge

Until your next turn:

- Attacks against you have disadvantage
    
- Dexterity saves have advantage
    

---

## Help

Assist an ally.

Usually grants advantage on a related check or attack.

---

## Hide

Attempt a Stealth check.

---

## Ready

Prepare an action to trigger later.

---

## Search

Look for hidden creatures, objects, or clues.

---

## Use an Object

Interact with an item requiring significant effort.

---

# Movement

Most creatures have:

```text
30 feet Speed
```

Movement can be divided throughout your turn.

Example:

```text
Move 15 feet
Attack
Move 15 feet
```

Difficult Terrain costs:

```text
2 feet of movement
for every 1 foot traveled
```

# Cover

## Half Cover

Examples:

- Low walls
    
- Furniture
    

Benefit:

```text
+2 AC
+2 Dexterity Saves
```

---

## Three-Quarters Cover

Examples:

- Arrow slits
    
- Large obstacles
    

Benefit:

```text
+5 AC
+5 Dexterity Saves
```

---

## Total Cover

The target cannot be directly targeted.

# Death Saves

When reduced to 0 Hit Points:

You fall unconscious and begin making Death Saving Throws.

At the start of each turn:

```text
Roll 1d20
```

---

## Success

```text
10–20
```

Mark one success.

---

## Failure

```text
1–9
```

Mark one failure.

---

## Natural 20

Immediately regain:

```text
1 HP
```

---

## Natural 1

Counts as:

```text
2 Failures
```

---

## Three Successes

Become stable.

---

## Three Failures

Die.

---

# Short Rest

Duration:

```text
1 Hour
```

During a Short Rest:

- Spend Hit Dice
    
- Recover class features
    
- Recover certain abilities
    

Must avoid strenuous activity.

---

# Long Rest

Duration:

```text
8 Hours
```

Benefits:

- Restore all Hit Points
    
- Recover spent Hit Dice
    
- Recover most class abilities
    
- Recover spell slots
    

Only one Long Rest generally counts per day.

# Spellcasting

## Spell Slots

Spellcasters expend Spell Slots to cast spells.

Higher-level spells usually require higher-level slots.

---

## Spell Components

Some spells require:

### Verbal (V)

Spoken words.

---

### Somatic (S)

Hand gestures.

---

### Material (M)

Physical components.

---

## Concentration

Some spells require Concentration.

You may only concentrate on:

```text
ONE
```

spell at a time.

Taking damage may force a Constitution Saving Throw to maintain concentration.

---

## Spell Save DC

When a spell forces a save:

```text
8
+ Proficiency Bonus
+ Spellcasting Ability Modifier
```

---

## Spell Attack Bonus

When making a spell attack:

```text
Proficiency Bonus
+ Spellcasting Ability Modifier
```

# Leveling Up

Characters gain levels through story progression and achievements determined by the DM.

When leveling up:

## Increase Maximum Hit Points

Gain additional Hit Points based on your class.

---

## Gain Class Features

Most levels grant:

- New abilities
    
- Improved abilities
    
- Additional resources
    

---

## Improve Spellcasting

Spellcasters may gain:

- New spells
    
- Additional spell slots
    
- More powerful magic
    

---

## Ability Score Improvements

Certain levels allow:

- Increase Ability Scores
    
- Select a Feat

# The Choosing Overview


## Timeline

| Phase                  | What happens                                        |
| :--------------------- | :-------------------------------------------------- |
| **Death of monarch**   | Kingdom enters **The Silence** (3 days)             |
| **Day 3 dawn**         | [[The Crown]] awakens; thirteen names on inner band |
| **Procession**         | Candidates travel with family, allies, crowds       |

---

## The Silence (Outside)

- Courts closed; wars pause when possible; no major laws; no public celebration.
- Tension: nobody predicts the list successfully.

---

## Inside: First Night

**[[The First Table]]** — non-magical, non-evaluative tradition. Everyone cares; Crown may or may not.

---

## Candidate Count

- **13 total** — 5–6 PCs, remainder NPCs ([[NPC Roster]]).
- Refusal: culturally unthinkable, not magical compulsion.

# Core Structure

Each cycle (roughly 3–5 in-game days) becomes:

1. Crown Trial
2. Nominations
3. Secondary Trial
4. Veto Ceremony
5. Elimination Vote
6. Departure Ceremony

Repeat until finalists.

---

# Stage 1: Crown Trial

The Hall announces a challenge.

Examples:

- Leadership challenge
- Negotiation challenge
- Resource management challenge
- Military simulation
- Public speaking trial
- Moral dilemma
- Team challenge

Winner becomes:

## Steward of the Hall

Benefits:

- Private chamber
- Access to records
- Better food
- Meetings with Hall officials
- Cannot be nominated that round

Most importantly:

### Names Two Candidates

Not for execution.

Not for exile.

For **Judgment**.

The Steward declares:

> "I place Lady Seraphine and Fenwick before Judgment."

These become nominees.

---

# Stage 2: Political Fallout

This is where the roleplay explodes.

Everyone immediately starts:

- lobbying
- bargaining
- forming alliances
- spreading rumors
- making promises

---

# Stage 3: Trial of Intervention 

A smaller challenge.

Participants:

- Steward
- Nominees
- Randomly selected candidates

Winner receives:

## The Writ of Intervention

They may:

### Save a Nominee

or

### Leave Nominations Unchanged

If someone is saved:

The Steward must nominate another candidate.

Chaos ensues.

---

# Stage 4: Deliberation

A formal ceremony.

Candidates speak publicly.

Think:

Big Brother nomination speeches mixed with a royal parliament.

Everyone makes arguments.

Examples:

- loyalty
- competence
- honor
- popularity
- lies

---

# Stage 5: The Bell Vote

Each candidate enters a chamber alone.

A bell hangs from the ceiling.

They strike it once while naming:

> "I vote to remove Fenwick."


---

# Stage 6: Departure

The eliminated candidate leaves the Hall.

Once Eliminated, players proceed to [[Shadows of the Hall]]

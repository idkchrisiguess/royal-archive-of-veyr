# The Hall of Saint Vaelor

> _"Thirteen enter beneath its bells. One leaves wearing the Crown."_

The Hall of Saint Vaelor stands upon the cliffs overlooking the Sea of Glass, its towers visible for miles across the northern coast of Veyr.

Older than many noble houses and older than the current royal line itself, the Hall serves as the sacred seat of the Choosing — the ancient tradition through which the Crown selects a new monarch.

For most of the realm's history, the Hall remains closed.

Its gates open only when the throne stands vacant.

Then the bells ring.

Then the candidates come.

---

# Overview

The Hall is not a palace.

It is not a fortress.

It is not a temple.

Yet it resembles all three.

Built from pale gray stone veined with silver, the structure combines royal architecture, sacred symbolism, and defensive construction into a sprawling complex of chambers, courtyards, libraries, gardens, galleries, and hidden passages.

Generations of rulers have lived, debated, failed, and changed within these walls.

Every monarch chosen by the Crown spent part of their life here.

No one knows exactly how old the Hall truly is.

Some historians claim portions of the foundations predate the Kingdom of Veyr itself.

---

# First Impressions

Most candidates describe the Hall as:

- Larger than expected
    
- Quieter than expected
    
- Older than expected
    

The air carries the scent of old paper, stone dust, candle wax, and ocean rain.

Footsteps echo strangely through many corridors.

Certain rooms seem larger on the inside than they should be.

Others feel smaller.

Visitors often report a persistent sensation that the Hall is listening.

No evidence has ever confirmed this.

No evidence has ever disproven it.

---

# Purpose of the Hall

The Hall exists to serve three functions:

## 1. The Choosing

The Hall houses every generation's Choosing.

Candidates live together within its walls while competing for the throne.

Challenges, ceremonies, debates, and votes all occur here.

---

## 2. Preservation of History

The Hall contains centuries of:

- Royal records
    
- Candidate journals
    
- Laws
    
- Correspondence
    
- Maps
    
- Genealogies
    

The archives beneath the Library are considered among the most complete historical collections in the kingdom.

---

## 3. Reflection

Many traditions of the Hall are designed not to test strength but character.

The Hall contains numerous locations intended for:

- Solitude
    
- Meditation
    
- Confession
    
- Debate
    
- Reconciliation
    

The Crown has never explained why these spaces are considered important.

---

# Architecture

The Hall was expanded repeatedly over five centuries.

As a result, its layout often feels inconsistent.

Wide ceremonial chambers connect to narrow stone corridors.

Elegant gardens border austere archives.

Ancient foundations support newer additions.

No complete architectural blueprint has ever been found.

Some claim one never existed.

# Important Locations

## [[Bell Tower]]

The highest point of the Hall.

The bells ring only during moments of national significance:

- Beginning of a Choosing
    
- Selection of a monarch
    
- Death of a reigning ruler
    

Most candidates visit the tower at least once.

Many leave thoughtful.

Some leave frightened.

---

## [[Thirteen Doors Corridor]]

A long corridor lined with thirteen identical doors.

Each candidate is assigned one.

The symbolism is obvious.

The meaning is not.

Many traditions begin here.

---

## [[The Commons]]

The social heart of the Hall.

Candidates gather here between ceremonies and challenges.

Most alliances begin here.

Many rivalries begin here as well.

---

## [[The First Table Hall]]

A grand chamber containing a circular table with thirteen seats.

No chair occupies the head of the table.

Tradition states:

> "The Crown sits at the head."

No one laughs when this is said.

---

## [[Grand Atrium]]

The largest room in the Hall.

Public announcements, ceremonies, and formal gatherings occur here.

The ceiling rises several stories overhead, supported by towering pillars carved with the names of past monarchs.

---

## [[Library of Vaelor]]

The greatest collection of historical knowledge in the kingdom.

The Library contains:

- Royal records
    
- Candidate journals
    
- Accounts of previous Choosings
    
- Histories of noble houses
    

Many candidates spend late nights here searching for answers.

Few find the answers they expected.

---

## [[Whisper Gallery]]

A narrow chamber attached to the Library.

Acoustics allow conversations spoken against one wall to be heard clearly on the opposite side.

Whether this was intentional remains debated.

The room has become associated with secrets, conspiracies, and private meetings.

---

## [[Kitchens and Hearth]]

The Hall's kitchens never close during a Choosing.

Meals are communal.

Tradition discourages candidates from eating alone.

The attached Hearth serves as one of the few places where rank and status temporarily lose importance.

---

## [[The Hearth Below]]

A lower chamber beneath the kitchens.

Warm, dimly lit, and rarely formal.

Many friendships are forged here.

Some political careers end here.

---

## [[Gardens of Reflection]]

A series of enclosed gardens within the Hall's central grounds.

Fountains, statues, and winding paths create an atmosphere of quiet contemplation.

Many candidates come here after difficult challenges.

---

## [[content/World/Locations/The Quiet Room|The Quiet Room]]

A simple chamber containing little more than chairs, candles, and silence.

Conversation is discouraged.

Reflection is expected.

No one is required to enter.

Most eventually do.

---

## [[Candidate Quarters]]

Living quarters assigned to the current generation of candidates.

Rooms vary slightly but are designed to prevent displays of wealth or status.

Within these walls, all candidates are intended to begin as equals.

Whether they remain equals is another matter.

---

## [[Crown Chamber]]

The most heavily guarded room in the Hall.

The Crown rests here between reigns.

Candidates are not permitted entry without invitation.

No invitation has ever been explained publicly.

---

# Daily Life During the Choosing

Life within the Hall follows a structured rhythm.

## Morning

- Announcements
    
- Briefings
    
- Ceremonial obligations
    

## Afternoon

- Challenges
    
- Investigations
    
- Political maneuvering
    
- Social encounters
    

## Evening

- Formal dinners
    
- Debates
    
- Private meetings
    

## Night

The Hall becomes quieter.

Not necessarily peaceful.

---

# The Hall's Reputation

Across Veyr, opinions about the Hall vary.

### Among Common Citizens

The Hall is viewed with reverence and curiosity.

Stories about its traditions are widespread.

---

### Among Nobility

The Hall is respected, feared, and studied.

Every noble family wishes to understand how the Crown chooses rulers.

None have succeeded completely.

---

### Among Former Candidates

Few speak openly about their experiences.

Those who do often describe the Hall not as a place but as a turning point in their lives.

---

# Rumors

The following stories circulate among candidates every generation:

- Secret passages connect every major chamber.
    
- The Hall changes slightly between Choosings.
    
- Hidden journals from previous candidates remain undiscovered.
    
- The Crown watches through the Hall itself.
    
- Certain rooms appear only when needed.
    
- Some doors cannot be opened twice.
    

No rumor has ever been conclusively verified.

---

# The Six Truths

> The Crown chooses.
> 
> The Choosing reveals.
> 
> Power changes all.
> 
> No ruler reigns alone.
> 
> Every reign ends.
> 
> The realm remembers.

Many candidates arrive believing the Hall exists to find a ruler.

Most leave realizing it was trying to reveal one.
